Facilitator Guide

Run rounds · live feeds · badges
As a Facilitator you run live events: start, stop, and schedule the rounds that already exist, watch what players are doing through the Live Answer Feed and Query Feed, and hand out the discretionary badges. You cannot create or delete rounds, author challenges or scenarios, manage indicators, edit configuration, or manage users — those stay with administrators. Your tools are in the Facilitator menu in the sidebar, and every badge action is written to the Audit Log.
Start, stop & schedule rounds

Open Manage Rounds to control any existing round. Use Timer to schedule its window — pick a start time (leave blank to begin now) and either a duration or a specific end time; answers submitted near the start earn up to 2× points, and none are accepted after the end. Use Enable / Disable to turn a round's timer on or off on the fly.

You can also download a round's solve log and score summary as CSV. Creating brand-new rounds and deleting rounds are administrator-only, so you can't accidentally remove a session mid-event.

Open Manage Rounds
Watch the live feeds

The Live Answer Feed shows every challenge submission in real time — who submitted what, whether it was correct, and which round it belongs to — with anti-cheat flags surfaced as they're detected. The Query Feed shows the KQL each player is running, so you can spot who's stuck and notice possible answer-sharing.

Both views are read-only and auto-refresh; you can scope them to a single round. The Query Feed appears only when the embedded KQL console is enabled.

Award badges

Most badges are earned automatically as players solve challenges and find indicators. On the Badges page you grant the discretionary ones — MVP, sportsmanship, team player — by hand, and revoke one if it was given by mistake.

Every grant and revoke is written to the Audit Log, so nothing here is silent.

Open Badges
Running a smooth event:
  • Schedule each round's window ahead of time, then just Enable it when you're ready to start.
  • Keep the Live Answer Feed open during play to catch stuck teams and anti-cheat flags early.
  • Save badges for the end, and always write a clear reason in chat — the grant itself is audit-logged.
