Grader Guide

Grading + monitoring
As a Grader you can do everything an Observer can (the monitoring views — Analytics, Live Answer Feed, Run History, Audit Log; see the Observer Guide), plus the grading tools below. You cannot start/stop the game, edit the scenario or configuration, manage users/teams, or take backups. Your tools are in the Facilitator menu in the sidebar. Every grading action is written to the Audit Log and is reconcilable by the Score Audit, so nothing you do here is silent or unrecoverable.
Answer Tester — adjudicate before you act

Before crediting or rejecting a disputed submission, check how the grader actually scores it. Pick a challenge (or paste accepted answers), enter the player's trial submission, and see whether it would be marked correct or wrong, with a per-alternate breakdown of each accepted answer's normalized form and which one matched.

It uses the exact same defang/normalization the live scoreboard uses, so hxxp://1[.]2[.]3[.]4/ correctly matches 1.2.3.4. Use this first to settle "but my answer was right!" disputes objectively.

Open Answer Tester
Re-grade a challenge

When a challenge's accepted answers were too strict (a typo, a missing valid form) and have since been fixed, re-grading retroactively credits players who submitted a now-correct answer earlier but were marked wrong. The Re-grade button previews exactly who would be credited and how many points before you apply it.

Each player is scored at their original attempt time, so an early submission keeps its speed bonus. The change is audited and fully reconcilable by the Score Audit.

Open Re-grade
Adjust a score

Manually add or subtract points for a team or player, with a required reason — for awarding a bonus, applying a penalty, or correcting a mistake. Use a negative number to subtract.

Each adjustment is written to the Audit Log and folded into the Score Audit's rebuild, so the running totals stay reconcilable. Prefer Re-grade when the issue is a challenge that should have accepted an answer; use an adjustment for one-off corrections and bonuses.

Open Adjust Score
Good grading habits:
  • Confirm with the Answer Tester before crediting a disputed answer.
  • Reach for Re-grade (not a manual adjustment) when a whole challenge was mis-graded — it credits everyone affected, at the right time.
  • Always write a clear reason on adjustments; it shows up in the Audit Log.
